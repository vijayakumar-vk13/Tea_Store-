Tea leaves are handpicked,only the tender top leaves and buds are selected.
Collected leaves are spread out and allowed to wither.This reduces moisture.
Withered leaves are rolled to break their cell walls, releasing essential oils.
Leaves are left to oxidize. Exposure to air changes their color and flavor.
Dried leaves are sorted by size. Finer leaves are often reserved for premium teas, while larger ones are used for blends.
The sorted leaves are ground into a fine powder. This is what we know as tea powder.
